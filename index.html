Public Health Analyst


Shalini Nair (SHAH-lynn-ee; she/her/hers) is an early career public health professional currently working at the intersection of infectious disease and health policy.

Passionate about prevention efforts, outbreak response, and public health infrastructure, Shalini currently serves as an Analyst for Infectious Disease at the Association of State and Territorial Health Officials (ASTHO). In this position, she supports a diverse portfolio of special projects addressing HIV, hepatitis, STIs, and COVID-19 across the 50 U.S. states, five territories, and three freely associated states. Through these projects, Shalini helps facilitate the collection of relevant information and experiences, create dialogue with outside organizations, represent the states at national forums on health, and identify best practices in public health.

In her earlier career, Shalini completed her Bachelor of Public Health and Master of Public Health in Epidemiology, both from the University of Florida (Go Gators!). During her time as an undergraduate, Shalini was a two-time recipient of the prestigious University Scholars fellowshipâ€”first as a general scholar and subsequently as a UF Health Cancer Center scholar. Shalini was also accepted into the competitive combined degree program, allowing her to begin coursework towards her graduate degree two years before formally matriculating into the graduate school. As part of her final internship experience, she worked at the Florida Department of Health assisting on the COVID-19 vaccination response. At the close of her time at UF, she was awarded the 2021 Outstanding MPH in Epidemiology student award for academic achievement shortly before beginning her career with ASTHO.

As a firm believer that learning is a lifelong process, Shalini enjoys coding (this website), exercising, and furthering her knowledge. Follow along on LinkedIn to see what she's up to!
